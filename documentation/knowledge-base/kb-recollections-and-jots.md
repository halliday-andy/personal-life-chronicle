# Recollections, jots & write-ups

*Knowledge base article · seeded 2026-07-19 · covers where memories
attach, the jot working-list model, and the ✍ write-up.*

## Where does a memory live?

Every recollection attaches to something, and choosing the right home
keeps it findable:

| The memory is about… | Write it… |
|---|---|
| **A place** (a home, an office, a vacation spot) | On that pin — "Your memory of it" when creating, or the pin's Edit panel |
| **A whole journey** (the drive, the voyage) | On the **trip** — via the trip's jots and ✍ write-up (globe trip strip or Travel Journal card) |
| **One stop along a journey** | On that **stop's own pin** |
| **A person** | On their page — "Add recollection" |
| Nothing on the map | Tell the capture assistant; it lives in your memories and can be linked to people and places afterward |

Attach to the most specific thing the memory is *about*. A night in
Winnipeg during a cross-country drive belongs to the Winnipeg stop; the
story of the drive as a whole belongs to the trip.

## Jots: a working list, not a set of contracts

A **jot** is one remembered thing, captured in seconds so it isn't lost:
"the whiteout on the pass," "Rick's broken binding," "the night in
Winnipeg." Every pin, trip, and person has a jot list ("Memories to
write"), and they behave like a good notebook:

- **One thought per jot.** Pasting several lines splits them into
  separate jots automatically — brain-dump freely.
- **Jots are not promises.** Nothing nags. They wait for as long as your
  elaboration takes — years is normal, and fine.
- **A jot need not become its own recollection.** Write one grand
  recollection that covers five highlights, then simply **check the
  five off**. Or write each up separately. The list serves you, not the
  other way around.
- **Jots can be an outline.** For a long trip, jotting the stops worth
  telling makes the list a table of contents for the elaboration to come.

## The ✍ write-up: from jot to recollection, guaranteed

Each open jot carries **✍ write**. Tapping it opens the capture
assistant already holding that jot and its host — tell the story in your
own words, and when it saves, the recollection is attached to the host
**deterministically**: a write-up can never end up orphaned or filed
under the wrong place, even if names are ambiguous. This is the most
reliable way to attach a memory to exactly the thing you meant.

(You can always just open the assistant and talk instead — it will
classify and file what you tell it — but for a memory that must land on
a specific trip or pin, the jot's ✍ write is the guaranteed path.)

## Recollection or context? Two kinds of content

- A **recollection** is *your* memory in *your* words. It is preserved
  verbatim, forever — the chronicle never rewrites it. Corrections layer
  on top; the original always survives underneath.
- A **context note** is background *about* something — pasted research,
  a history of the base you lived on, an article about the ski area.
  Context attaches to the person/place/organization it describes and
  frames every memory that mentions them.

Paste research into the capture assistant and it will propose it as
context on the right entity (you approve before anything saves). Tell it
a memory and it becomes a recollection. Mixed submissions get split, each
part to its proper home.

## Fixing things afterward

- **Edit a saved recollection** from your memories page — the earlier
  wording is kept as a revision, never destroyed.
- **Wrong or missing people/places on a memory?** The memory's entity
  links are editable — unlink the wrong one, type-ahead the right one.
  The prose never has to change to fix the connections.
