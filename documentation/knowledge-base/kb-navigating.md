# Finding your way around

*Knowledge base article · seeded 2026-07-19 · covers globe search, the
two map looks, the line language, and moving between surfaces.*

## Searching: your pins first, then the world

The globe's search box answers two different intents in one place:

- **Your pins** — type a few letters of anything you've named and your
  own places rank first: homes lead, marked "· not yet placed" when they
  haven't joined your sequence, with each pin's era phrase shown so
  repeat places disambiguate. Picking one **flies to and opens it** — no
  zooming and hunting, no detour through the Journey page.
- **Places** — the world's places list beneath, for pinning somewhere
  new. Picking one flies there and starts a draft pin, exactly as
  placing has always worked.
- **Coordinates** — paste a `lat, lng` pair (straight from another map
  app) and a "Go to…" row takes you there.

If you're navigating, pick from *Your pins*; if you're adding, pick from
*Places*. That distinction also protects you from accidentally creating
a duplicate of a place you already have.

## Why the map changes when you zoom in

The globe has two deliberate looks:

- **The dark globe** at world and regional scale — your life as the
  bright layer: the glowing thread of homes, the ember pins, the quiet
  world beneath.
- **The detailed map** up close — when you're placing or reading a
  specific spot, the view dissolves into a full-detail map: buildings,
  trails, lifts, street names. Legibility takes over where precision
  matters.

The crossover happens automatically around town-level zoom, in both
directions. Nothing about your data changes — it's the same globe
wearing reading glasses.

## The line language

- **The glowing thread** — your home sequence, in order. The brightest
  thing on the globe, always.
- **Solid cool line** — a commute: home to workplace.
- **Dashed faint line** — an association: a vacation, stay, or Log tied
  to its home era.
- **Rose route arcs** — trips: solid outward, dashed back (one-way trips
  draw no return). Hidden behind the **Trips** toggle so the thread
  stays dominant; a selected trip always shows its route.

The default view is deliberately spare — just the thread. Hover a pin to
peek at its side lines; use the legend's filters or "Side lines in view"
(available once you're zoomed into a region) to reveal more.

## Moving between surfaces

Every surface hands off to the others without losing your place:

- **Globe → Journey:** a pin's card offers "Read in journey →", landing
  on that exact stop, expanded.
- **Journey → Globe:** every stop offers "Show on globe →"; the globe
  arrives with the pin selected and framed.
- **Travel Journal ↔ Globe:** trip cards link "Show on globe →" and
  "Edit on globe"; the globe's trip strip links back into the Journal.
- **Anything → its memories:** cards link to the exact recollection on
  your memories page, and mentions of a person or place link to that
  entity's own page.

The address bar keeps up with where you are, so a link copied mid-journey
drops someone (or future you) at the same spot.
