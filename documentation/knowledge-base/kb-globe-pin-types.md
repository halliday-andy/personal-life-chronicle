# Pin types & the anchor question

*Knowledge base article · seeded 2026-07-19 · covers the globe's "A place
in your life" modal.*

## What kind of place? — the eight choices

When you drop a pin, the type you choose tells the globe what role the
place played:

- **Primary residence** — a home you lived in. These form the main line
  of your life's journey (the glowing thread). Everything else hangs off
  them.
- **Second residence** — a home you kept *alongside* your main one: a
  lake house, a seasonal place you returned to.
- **Short-term stay** — somewhere you lived briefly: a summer, a sublet,
  a short posting.
- **Workplace** — an office or employer. Draws a commute line to the home
  you lived in then.
- **Vacation** — a place you traveled to for leisure — a visit, not a home.
- **Professional travel** — a work trip to a place you didn't live.
- **Log** — a place worth marking simply because something memorable
  happened there. It makes no category claim, and it can associate with
  *any* of your pins — a home, a vacation, anywhere.
- **Future place** — somewhere you *want* to go or live one day.
  Aspiration, not history; promote it to a real type when you've been.
- **Trip — somewhere I traveled** — starts a Trip: the pin becomes the
  trip's destination. See [Trips & travel](kb-trips.md).

**Not sure which?** Log is the safe choice — it claims nothing and can be
re-typed later without losing anything you've written.

## "Which home were you living in then?" — what the anchor really asks

Most non-home pins ask you to pick a home. This question is about
**which era of your life the place belongs to** — not where you returned
to afterward, and not a judgment about the place itself. Picking a home:

- draws a dashed line tying the place to that home on the globe,
- files the place (and its memories) under that home's chapter of your
  life, so browsing the home surfaces them.

The list offers every **home** you've pinned: your primary residences —
including ones marked "not yet placed" in your sequence — plus second
residences and short-term stays. A home that isn't in your sequence yet
is still a home; it appears with a "· not yet placed" note.

**"Not sure / standalone"** is always available. A standalone pin keeps
everything (memories, photos, jots) — it just isn't tied to an era yet.
You can pick an anchor later from the pin's Edit panel.

**Logs are the exception:** a Log can anchor to *any* pin, not only
homes — "the lighthouse we found near the beach house" anchors to the
vacation, not to a home.

## Common cases

**"I was between homes when this happened."** Pick the home you'd just
left — the era question reads naturally as "which chapter was this part
of," and the chapter you were leaving is usually the honest answer. (For
a journey between homes, consider framing it as a Trip — see
[Trips & travel](kb-trips.md).)

**"This place already exists in my chronicle."** If the name matches
something you've mentioned before, the modal offers *"this looks like
your existing X — pin it instead of creating a duplicate?"* Accepting
keeps all the earlier mentions attached to the one place. Prefer that
over creating a twin.

**"I picked the wrong type."** Edit the pin and change its type. Your
recollection, photos, and jots always survive a re-type. If you re-type a
home *out* of being a primary residence, places anchored to it become
standalone — re-anchor them where they now belong (or convert them to
Logs anchored wherever fits).

## The small print on the pin

- **Name on the pin** — yours to edit; the search suggestion is only a
  starting point.
- **When?** — a free phrase in your own words ("early 70s", "1959 to
  1960"). The chronicle never converts your phrase into dates behind
  your back.
- **Placard** — one glanceable line shown when someone hovers the pin
  ("My first CEO desk").
