# The residential spine — your sequence of homes

*Knowledge base article · seeded 2026-07-19 · covers primary residences,
sequencing, and "Decide later — not yet placed."*

## What the spine is

Your primary residences, in the order you lived in them, form the
chronicle's backbone — the glowing thread on the globe and the stops on
your Journey page. The first home wears the origin star; the last reads
"now." Everything else in the chronicle hangs off this scaffold, which is
why completing it — birth to now, every home — is the most valuable
early thing you can do. It's also genuinely achievable in weeks; the
*stories* are a years-long pleasure, but the skeleton is not.

The spine records **order**, not dates. "Which home came after which" is
something you know with certainty even when years are fuzzy — so that is
all the sequence ever asks of you.

## "Decide later — not yet placed"

Pick this whenever you know it was a home but not yet where it fits —
common when a burst of memories surfaces homes out of order, or when a
trip reminds you of a home that predates everything you've pinned.

A not-yet-placed home is a **full citizen**: write its recollection, add
photos, jot memories, set its "when" phrase, anchor workplaces and
vacations to it. Only the *order-dependent* things wait:

- it doesn't join the glowing thread (no arcs in or out),
- it can't be the origin star or the "now" endpoint,
- it sits in its own **"Not yet placed"** group on the Journey page,
- on the globe it wears a slowly turning dashed ring that says
  "unfinished."

**To place it later:** open the pin → Edit (or the detail card's "Place
in sequence →") → the same picker → choose its slot. It joins the thread
and everything anchored to it comes along.

**To unplace a sequenced home:** the same picker offers "Decide later"
on an already-sequenced home too — it steps off the thread, the
remaining homes close ranks, and nothing you wrote is lost.

## Things people wonder

**"Will choosing 'Decide later' lose my work when I place it?"** No.
Placement only assigns the slot. Recollection, photos, facts, jots, and
anchored places all persist through place / unplace / re-place.

**"I lived in two places at once."** The spine wants your *primary*
thread. Make one the primary residence and the other a **Second
residence** anchored to it — that's exactly what the type is for.

**"I moved back to a place I'd lived before."** Pin it again as a new
primary residence for the second stint — same place on the map, its own
stop in the sequence. Repeat eras are separate chapters.

**"How exact does the location have to be?"** As exact as you like, and
never final: select the pin → "Refine location" → drag → save. Only the
map point moves; nothing else changes.
